Featured Nature Reserves

Hluhluwe Imfolozi Game Reserve

Hluhluwe Imfolozi Game Reserve is the oldest proclaimed nature reserve in Africa. It covers 96,000 hectares and is renowned for its rich wildlife and conservation efforts.

Key features:

  • Home to the Big Five (lion, leopard, rhino, elephant, buffalo)
  • World-famous for its white rhino conservation program
  • Diverse landscapes from savannah to forests
  • Self-drive and guided safari options
  • Historical significance as the birthplace of wilderness conservation

The reserve offers excellent game viewing opportunities throughout the year and is particularly known for its rhino sightings.

Paradise Valley Nature Reserve

Located just outside Durban, Paradise Valley Nature Reserve offers a tranquil escape with beautiful waterfalls, lush vegetation, and diverse birdlife.

Key features:

  • Stunning waterfall that cascades into a natural pool
  • Well-maintained hiking trails through indigenous forest
  • Abundant birdlife including Knysna Turacos and Paradise Flycatchers
  • Picnic spots with beautiful views
  • Accessible boardwalks for easy viewing

The reserve is perfect for day trips and offers a peaceful retreat from urban life while being easily accessible from Durban.
